# A Quick Primer on Impact Evaluation Methods

Once it is the right time and resources are in place, you must choose a method. At its core, an impact evaluation compares outcomes between groups that differ only in exposure to the intervention—that is, treatment versus control. There are several ways to achieve or approximate this:

Randomization ensures groups are comparable on average, except for the intervention. Sometimes, politics, ethics, or other constraints will make it less feasible to randomize, so we can turn to other methods.</td><td><a href="https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1588348442528-85c6fa3b0440?crop=entropy&#x26;cs=srgb&#x26;fm=jpg&#x26;ixid=M3wxOTcwMjR8MHwxfHNlYXJjaHw1fHxleHBlcmltZW50fGVufDB8fHx8MTc3MzI4NDgxOXww&#x26;ixlib=rb-4.1.0&#x26;q=85">https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1588348442528-85c6fa3b0440?crop=entropy&#x26;cs=srgb&#x26;fm=jpg&#x26;ixid=M3wxOTcwMjR8MHwxfHNlYXJjaHw1fHxleHBlcmltZW50fGVufDB8fHx8MTc3MzI4NDgxOXww&#x26;ixlib=rb-4.1.0&#x26;q=85</a></td></tr><tr><td><strong>Propensity score matching</strong></td><td>This approach requires a large dataset covering both participants and non-participants, with a clear indicator of treatment.
